# data_science_methodology
resources for data science projects based on a data science lifecycle methodology

In 2021, a pre-print of [Data Science Methodologies: Current Challenges and Future Approaches](https://doi.org/10.1016/j.bdr.2020.100183), Martinez et al. (2021), was published to the arXiv. This work compares nineteen different frameworks and provides a roadmap for development of an updated data science methodology, with the overall position that the purpose of such a methodology is to address challenges and pain points encountered by data science practitioners working on projects. Another theme of the work is that CRISP-DM (published in 1990) was due for an updated data-science-centric treatment.  

Given previous experience using a CRISP-DM based approach for Quality Assurance in data science project work and an interest in how data science teams do continuous improvement, the matters discussed were of interest and the article served as a foundation for developing my ideas in this area. After examining as many of the nineteen frameworks as I could track down, I consolidated my research into some new resources that teams can use.

There are various similarities and differences among the nineteen frameworks surveyed, but the majority have a a **data science lifecycle** as the core concept. For this reason I attempted to fit all the notes I took on each of the frameworks into one overall lifecycle document.  It can be found here as dslc.md

Another influence on this work was The Checklist by Atul Gawande, a book that discusses how to get things right in complex environments such as hospitals, aviation, or large-scale construction. Along these lines my hope was to provide a resource allowing new practitioners to navigate doing a data science project for the first time and allowing experienced practitioners  to check they have covered off everything they usually need to consider. 

With the backbone of a lifecycle in place, many other tools and artefacts can be built around it or integrated with it (depending on the needs of the team). Where I have resources suitable for open source along these lines I may add them to the repository in the future.
